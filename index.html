The Venue

We are looking forward to celebrate with you in one of Vienna's Baroque jewels, the Palais Daun-Kinsky. Built between 1713 and 1719 by Johann Lucas von Hildebrandt, who was also the architect of the Belvedere Palace, it has been home to various noble families, served as police headquarters, an embassy, and hosted international negotiations, all to prepare for its most important event yet, our wedding celebrations.

Located in the first district, it is easy to reach both by public transport and car. It is a five-minute walk from both the metro stations Herrengasse (U3) and Schottentor (U2). The latter is serviced additionally by many tram lines. From Stephansplatz (U1), it is a twelve-minute walk. If you come by car, there is a paid underground garage at the square in front of the Palais

When you arrive at the venue take the main staircase to the left of the entrance to the second floor (there will be signs).

Our Recommendations in Vienna

Museums and Historical Sights in Vienna

If you want to see art specific to Austria, these museums are a great place to start:

  • Albertina: This museum focuses on classical art, including famous works like Dürer's "Young Hare," and often features changing exhibitions.
  • Upper Belvedere: Home to the most famous paintings by Gustav Klimt, including "The Kiss."
  • Leopold Museum: This museum houses a beautiful collection of Austrian artists from the Jugendstil (Art Nouveau) period around 1900.
  • Hofburg Palace, where you can see the Imperial Apartments and the Imperial Treasury and the World Museum.
  • Schönbrunn Palace: walk in the beautiful park and visit the Schönbrunn Zoo.
